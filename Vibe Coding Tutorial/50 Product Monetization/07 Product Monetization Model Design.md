## Necessity of Monetization Models

After understanding what a monetization model is, I'll use some small examples to share the necessity of monetization models with everyone.

Like me starting to do programming knowledge payment in '22, on one hand to give myself more motivation to help more friends learning programming, forcibly not letting myself be idle; on the other hand also want to accumulate resources for my future entrepreneurship.

## How to Choose a Monetization Model?

Monetization models can be as simple as a cup of plain water, or as complex as a specialty drink.

Because my experience is limited, I'll share some basic, mainstream monetization models with everyone, suitable for the vast majority of friends.



### 1. Direct Charging

Directly charge users a fixed fee in exchange for products or services.

Relatively common examples are software licenses (or game discs we bought when we were kids), you want to use this software, have to purchase its usage rights.

This model is suitable for providing products with **relatively fixed value**, must be extremely cautious when setting pricing, refer more to the market and comprehensively price based on your own product value.



### 2. Ad Revenue

Obtain revenue by directly providing ad display space to traffic owners or advertisers, is a monetization model with very low cost and risk.

For example adding an AI-related product recommendation position in our Fish Skin AI navigation page:

If our platform traffic itself is relatively large and has its own customers, then don't need to go through advertiser platforms, directly communicating with customers about specific ad charging models will be more profitable. Like charging according to website daily visits * days * ad position value.

But if platform traffic is unstable and itself doesn't have an ad statistics system, suggest connecting to third-party advertiser platforms, like Google AdSense. Not only don't need to connect with ad clients yourself, but can also very flexibly configure ad positions, view ad effect revenue, very suitable for individual website owners.

Need to note, for a developing product, must balance ad quantity and user experience, weigh the ratio of ad revenue and user loss.



### 3. Subscription Model

Let users purchase product or service usage rights according to certain time cycles.

For example the vast majority of products use monthly and yearly membership systems, is the most common subscription model.

This method is suitable for products or services that update frequently and can provide continuous value, like knowledge planets, information subscriptions, etc. Need to cultivate user usage habits, improve user loyalty, otherwise might not be as effective as direct charging models.



### 4. Freemium Model

Freemium (freeÂ¢ûÂÄº model), refers to products first providing basic functions or free services to attract users to use, then realizing profit through paidÂ¢ûÂÄº functions.

The downstream of this model might be direct charging or subscription models. The core idea lies in: first retain users through free services, then build user trust, cultivate user usage habits, then charge, conversion will be much better than direct "selling." Therefore this is also one of the most common models.

Our Fish Skin AI is this model, early stage consuming large amounts of cost to provide users with free services, equivalent to investing to seize users; later stage then converting and monetizing users.

But when using this method, must weigh free users vs paid users ratio, conversion rate and benefits, don't be like us and lose money in early stage from being harvested.



### 5. Commission/Transaction Fees

Platform acts as intermediary for transactions or services, collecting a certain proportion of fees from each completed transaction.

The most typical example is WeChat Pay, every transaction is equivalent to giving WeChat a "cut"; Knowledge Planet is also the same model.

The advantage of this method is low risk, large long-term returns, basicallyË∫∫ÁùÄËé∑Âà© in later stage; but the disadvantage is providing intermediate service system construction needs cost, must guarantee service quality, and early-stage obtaining customer trust cost difficulty is relatively large.



### 6. Licensing Model

License your own brand, technology or intellectual property to other enterprises or individuals for use, obtaining licensing fees.

SaaS (Software as a Service) can be seen as a licensing model, providing software products deployed on cloud servers to licensed users.

Of course, the downstream of licensing models can also be direct charging or subscription. Relatively suitable for enterprises that possess unique technology or intellectual property and have relatively high unit prices.



### 7. Outcome Sales

By collecting and analyzing own product's outcomes, and selling the outcomes to other enterprises to obtain revenue.

Outcomes here can be website content, website data, website operation analysis conclusions, etc.

For example, a certain travel website definitely has massive user travel interest preference data, another company making travel products might want to purchase this data to improve products. Like some AI drawing websites, can also package exquisite AI drawings and sell to companies researching AI drawing models or some image display websites.

This model is suitable for products that possess large amounts of valuable data, general small products can't do it. But when we develop systems, must always have such aÊ≤âÊ∑Ä awareness, keep all website data well, might have unexpected value someday.



### 8. Value-Added Services

Refers to providing additional services on the basis of original products or services, thereby increasing revenue sources.

Relatively typical example is Alipay, product itself provides payment services, but now you open Alipay and can see various life services like top-up, takeout, etc.

Like our Fish Skin AI website, also can completely provide AI technology consulting services as value-added services, just that our manpower is limited, so didn't do this.

Need to note, value-added services must match core products, rather than adding all kinds of miscellaneous services to the product, affecting user experience.



### 9. Partnership Model

A relatively advanced model, through cooperating with others, mutually letting each other profit, achieving mutual benefit and win-win.

There are many feasible cooperation methods. For example Product A provides job hunting services, Product B provides university student consulting services, these two products can completely mutually divert and recommend, increasing respective user bases; can also be Company A provides underlying technology, Company B provides upper-layer services, revenue sharing.

Need to note, choosing partners must simultaneously consider benefits the other party gives you and benefits you give the other party, establishing complete profit sharing mechanism. Because once benefits aren't equal, future cooperation will inevitably have conflicts.

Monetization model is the core of product monetization. Choosing the right monetization model can let products continuously generate value; choosing the wrong monetization model might make products difficult to sustain.

Remember these key points:

1. There are many types of monetization models, choose based on product characteristics
2. Can combine multiple monetization models, provide more choices
3. Must balance short-term revenue and long-term development
4. Must continuously optimize monetization models, adjust strategies based on data
5. Monetization models must align with user needs, can't only consider making money

In the Vibe Coding era, the threshold for making products is already very low. But, how to let products generate value and continuously develop, still needs you to seriously think about monetization models.
